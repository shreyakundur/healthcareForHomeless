Healthcare Statistics

  • More than 200 million children die worldwide under the age of 5 each year from lack of healthcare
  • 10 million people die annually from treatable illnesses such as diarrhea and pneumonia
  • 23,000 people in Alameda County experience homelessness in a given year -- more than 1% of the population.
  • People experiencing homelessness die from the same causes as people in the general population, but 20 to 30 years earlier.
  • Between 2014 and 2015, spending on prescription drugs grew by 9% to $325 billion. That's an alarmingly fast growth rate.
  • The number of uninsured Americans dropped by 3.8 million from January to March, which brought the average percentage of people without health insurance to 13.1 percent of the world.
